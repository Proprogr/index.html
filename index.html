What it means to live with a disability ?

Living as a disabled is very challenging but also learning in terms of how the society makes its approach with disabilty and disabled people in general, Sometimes, it's like those people may be similar to social barometers despite social bareers they are experiencing every single day. From disabled people, it may be possible to jauge the level of humility, sensibility, receptivity, proactivity and/or solidarity amongst many other qualities of people, based partly on their behavings. There is no doubt that being disabled is not easy and the level of difficulties varies from person to person and from a region to another. Depending the status and the region where disabled people are living, will determine the quality and life experiences. Many of you do know the existence of discriminations towards these people but it shouldn't. How tough it is for them to manage a lifetime to fulfill. That's why Tiki N'Yiki is implemented and is focused to improve Education for disabled kids and helps them realize their dreams.

Etymology and Meaning

Tiki N'Yiki is etymologically structured of two (2) words of Moore, a language in Burkina Faso :

1. Tiki is litteraly meaning Leaning upon

2. N'Yiki is meaning Getting up

Why Tiki N'Yiki ?

Tiki N'Yiki is created to improve education and support efforts of disabled children in Burkina Faso. It is sounding as a slogan adressing the necessity and the possibility for everyone to lead his own achievement.

Nothing Is Easy

It's not easy to take down a mindset of culture, thinking and behaving that have been formatted into social mindset that is usually considering disabled people are good for nothing. But it's little by little through channels like sensibilizing about disability, promoting positivity and creating opportunities to skilled disabled who deserve, improving education will make disability more apprehended. conceived, interesting and interested, game-changing and basically impacting. That's why taking down any kind of misconception, negativity surrounding disability is the main focus to reach. That could help to better understand disabled people, mitigate the undermining of their image in the communities. Many of them are skilled. Excluding them from a social development is shameful nowdays. We get to have approaches and conversations about disability and its related issues and then make sure everyone should get included. Making that is trying to core a culture of inclusiveness, generate confidence and make impact in the community. The important thing is to step into this question: What and why still causing the social bareers ? Perhaps finding the answer will bring up to light to issues including inclusion. And this is having on a long way. Thank you doing something to get inclusiveness more reallistic and benefit the people who deserve it.
