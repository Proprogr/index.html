What Tiki Yiki is Meaning ? What Tiki N'Yiki is Doing ?

In Burkina Faso as everywhere worldwide, there are many challenges about inclusiveness. Getting "differently abled people" included in the communities is not evident on a daily basis. But it's so important to give them opportunities they deserve even their disabilities. Some organisations are steadily working to get inclusiveness done.

"Inclusiveness" May Be Ordinary Or New To You. But Inclusiveness Is Worded In The Dictionary And Existed Since Its Creation. It Belongs To Human-Beings To Put It Into Practical Life. That's Why Inclusiveness in Education Also Matters. It's Not A Privilege But A Right For Every Kid Including Those Who Are Disabled And/Or Different.

Inclusive Education has no room everywhere. It should not be a privilege but an intrinsic right.

Health Care is a human basic right worldwide. But not everyone has it everywhere. In Africa some people are dying from diseases that can be avoided. It should'nt.

Burkina Faso, the land of integrity, a landlocked and low-income Sub-Saharan African country with limited natural resources, is administratively structured of 45 provinces and nested within the heart of West Africa. Burkina Faso was a former colony of France from which it gained (took) independence in August 5th, 1960. Its capital and main city is Ouagadougou populated by 1.086.505 people in the city area. The global population of Burkina Faso is about 23 300 000 inhabitants with a dominant percentage of youth. Geography influences Economy. The economy of Burkina Faso is mostly based on agriculture, livestock breeding, mining, market gardening. The informal sector is growing and believed to create more jobs. Citizens of Burkina Faso are called Burkinabè or Burkinabé but no a plural mark with 'S' even if it is at least two inhabitants.

Immigration Is Heartbreaking
As a physical disabled person who legally made it to Canada, I experienced many challenges on my way and still experiencing. In addition to racism and discrimination, seeking asylum in Montreal [Canada] is a long and difficult way to reach. It matters with hard work, rigor, ponctuality, credibility, a lot of energy and a dose of optimism to deal with longer and administrative procedures that can last at least after many years (5-6 years) before getting the citizenship if there is obviously a grant. It's been so hard because as a newcomer in a new country I have to embrace a new culture in addition to mine and then get adapted and integrated. I had neither parents, nor relatives in Quebec-Canada at this time and I have to find out a way and place to stay. After spending 3 days in my motel room my finances have been hit. Thereby I've had to look for a cheap room to live. But how to do it as a newcomer in one of the coldest countries around the world on an upcoming winter ? Something was sure, I couldn't afford to sleep outside. Then I went to OBM shelter in downtown Montreal. There I spent a half year and then I moved to "La Maison du Père" to wait for my hearing. I have successfully defended my case and got a grant. That's why I want to share some of my experiences with you in an upcoming book.

The vision of Tiki N'Yiki is promoted by Gabriel, a physical disabled person from Burkina Faso. He received his college degree at Bassy high school in Ziniaré and then moved to Ouagadougou at Newton high school. He holds a Patent of Undergraduate Studies (P.U.S) called B.E.P.C [Brevet d'Etude du Premier Cycle].
As a fervent defender of Disability Rights, Gabriel knew he has to stand up and get involved within activities about disablity and corollary matters by implementing ideas into practical realisations that can positively change lives. That's why he initiated the Centre PAAS NEERE for EDUCATION in 2006. Ideas are sometimes common even complexe and deep-thinking, but those linked frontally to disability and rights, are main subjects of interest for Gabriel.

The need to get results in terms of social impacts for disabled people and to the communities where they are living, is a huge focus that sharpens the dynamism of Gabriel. This is a way to promote, defend and protect Disability Rights and legacies that disabled activists have achieved, especially the right to get educated and be normally health-carried no matter who you are.

In 2006 ... Gabriel was one of the founding members of PAAS NEERE organisation, an educational organisation in which he taught disabled and orphaned children and led several awareness sessions with the focal theme as “Disability and Social Inclusion”. In 2007, 2008, 2013, Gabriel has toured cities in Europe in order to promote the organisation and fundraise money for important projects as education and health care. His stays (trips) led him not only to Switzerland like in Geneva, Sion, Delemont, and Vendlincourt with impactful collaboration of friends and founding members of Transport Handicap in the canton of Valais but also in Germany at Rasttat and Kuppenheim in the lander of Bade-Wurtemberg [Baden-Württemberg] where he host a serie of conferences about PAAS NEERE the organisation of which he was president.

On december 11th, 2012 Gabriel was awarded as " Knight of the Order of Merit " by his country, Burkina Faso for his entire contribution and engagement in terms of being able to create, propel and maintain an inclusive environment for vulnerable people. Disabled and orphaned children are benefiting of his master-piece and the centre Paas Neere is like an harbor for them to get better lives through education.

In 2016 he created Tiki N'Yiki to expand more support for orphaned and marginalized children. On July 14, 2016, Gabriel was invited by generous friends to the United States of America for four (4) months to not only networking but also to selling his artworks [Paintings & Batiks] and fundraise money for projects.

From East Coast [ NYC-NY, Boston-MA, Appleton-ME, Burlington & St Johnsbury-VT] to West Coast [ Portland-OR, Olympia-WA] of the United States of America, with a huge support of friends, Gabriel was not only able to present his vision-project of Tiki N'Yiki during festivals and/or fairs but also talked in person with some people about realities of what it means to be disabled in his country, Burkina Faso given some exclusive mentalities and existential discriminations. As future perspectives, his idea is to build a wider network of collaborators and partners gathered around a foundation to work hard, achieve projects, support empowerment and social inclusion for disabled people.

In 2017, unfortunately for security reasons, Gabriel has left his country to fly to Canada . In fact, some of Tiki N'Yiki activities, including EDUCATION, awareness on disability, have been temporarily stopped but will be redefined if the conditions of security are met.
He is rebuilding a new life but with a ceaseless desire to not give up his vision of Tiki Yiki. Among other things, he is writting his memoirs, learning to code while hoping to attend Informations and Technologies studies. He is passionate on programming and is developing this website to let well-known his vision.
Gabriel likes nature, photography, history, geography, architecture, cyclism, European soccer and hockey on ice.
