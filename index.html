What Tiki Yiki is Meaning ? What Tiki N'Yiki is Doing ?

In Burkina Faso as everywhere there is a lot of challenges about inclusiveness. Getting "differently able people especially disabled people" included in communities is not regular on the daily basis. But it's so important to give them opportunities even their disabilities. Some organisations are steadily working to get it done including to be trully inclusive.

"Inclusiveness" May Be Ordinary Or New To You. But Inclusiveness Is Worded In The Dictionary And Existed Since Its Creation. It Belongs To Human-Beings To Put It Into Practical Life. That's Why Inclusiveness in Education Also Matters. It's Not A Privilege But A Right For Every Kid Including Those Who Are Disabled And/Or Different.

Inclusive Education has no room everywhere. It should not be a privilege but an intrinsic right.

Health Care Is A Human Right Worldwide. But In Some Regions Around The World, People Are Dying Because Simply Of Malaria.

Health Care is a human basic right worldwide. But not everyone has it everywhere. In Africa some people are dying from diseases that can be avoided. It should'nt.

What Means Food Security? What Should Be Done To Fix Food Insecurity Worldwide?

While in some regions worldwide people have no more to worry about Food Security, in some other ones, it is unfortunately not the same reality. There are ceaselessly worries in terms of Food Security. They have to struggle hard to get needs met. In some worse cases, some died. But No Food Security, No Sustainable Development.

Climate Change Is a Big Threat To Earth And To Human-Beings. Climate Change is Deadly Causing Floodings, Wildfires, Droughts, Earthquakes And Volcanos Around The World. There Is No Doubt that Climate Change Has More Endangered Earth Than Never Before. But Human-Being Has Provoked It Through His Continual Agression of Mother Nature. Now He Has to Stand up and Figure It Out In Order to Mitigate The Danger [Threat].

Climate Change has neither been a story nor a comedy. It's a mad reality.

Disability Is Not Destiny. But For Disabled People Living In Exclusive Areas, They Have To Struggle Two Times Harder At Least Than Anyone To Get Their Needs And Achieve. They are experiencing Toxic Environments Favored By A Lot Of Stigmatisations.

As a disabled person, I experienced and still experiencing discriminations. It's too tough and challenging but It's Teaching . I have to find out a way to get my rights respected as human-being.

Culture Is Like A Backbone For Communities To Survive In The Biggest Family Of Diversity. It's A Way To Promote And Preserve Cultural Identity From Elders [Grand-Parents and Parents] To Kids or To Grand-Sons. Everyone Is Playing A Key Role Of Transmission In Order To Strongly Preserve It

Promoting Saxiwama as a cultural practice is a best way to preserve and transmit it.

Welcome in Burkina Faso

Burkina Faso is a landlocked country located in the heart of West Africa. Its capital and main city is Ouagadougou. The population of Burkina Faso is about 23 300 000 inhabitants mainly composed of young people. Geography influences Economy. The economy of Burkina Faso is mostly based on the agriculture, livestock breeding, mining, market gardening. The informal sector is believed to create many jobs for people. Citizens are called Burkinabè.

Immigration Is Heartbreaking
As a physical disabled person who legally made it to Canada, I experienced many challenges on my way. First of all, seeking asylum is a long and difficult process. It matters with longer administrative procedures that can end at least after 5 to 6 years before getting the citizenship if there is a grant. It's been so hard because as a disabled person in a new country I have to embrace a new culture and get adapted. I had neither parents, nor relatives in Quebec-Canada at this time and I have to find out a way to stay. After spending 3 days in my motel room my finances have been hit. Thereby I've had to look for a cheap room to live. But how to do it as a newcomer in one of the coldest countries around the world during winter time? Something was sure, I couldn't afford to sleep outside. Then I went to downtown Montreal in a shelter in order to get my situation fixed. I spent half and a year to have my hearing. Fortunately I have been granted and I applied for the permanent residency. So I wanted to share informations and experiences with anyone who is in need.Learn more...
