What it means to live with a disability ?

Living as a disabled person is tough, perpetually challenging and also life-changing. Over to experience some bit of consideration by the ones and a lot of discriminations by the others, living with a disabilty can be learning and teaching in some ways.

In terms of how disabled people are courageous and determinated to make their ways to live despite disability, is inspiring, energy-fueling and may hope-making for some people in the communities.

Disabled people are unintentionally often considered like social barometers in a spite of social bareers they are living. Through conversations and social interactions with the so-called "normal people", they are sometimes able to identify who is an inclusive-minded person or not based on views, approaches and behavings about disability.

As human-beings we should more advocate for universal values like solidarity, compassion, mutual respect, sharing spirit among others in order to build a society based on harmony, cohesion and inclusion for all.

Basically living as a disabled is very tough and the issues can vary from person to person and from a region to another. Wherever disabled people are living, will be impacting their lifetime experiences.

But it's not unknown that the harsh and silent climate of discriminations towards disabled people, is undermining their rights. They have always to live with unfairness. It shouldn't.

That's why Tiki N'Yiki is focused to improve Education and Protect Rights of disabled people generally but especially disabled children.

Nothing Is Easy

It's not easy to take down a mindset of culture, thinking and behaving that have been formatted into social mindset that is usually considering disabled people are good for nothing. But it's little by little through channels like sensibilizing about disability, promoting positivity and creating opportunities to skilled disabled who deserve, improving education will make disability more apprehended. conceived, interesting and interested, game-changing and basically impacting. That's why taking down any kind of misconception, negativity surrounding disability is the main focus to reach. That could help to better understand disabled people, mitigate the undermining of their image in the communities. Many of them are skilled. Excluding them from a social development is shameful nowdays. We get to have approaches and conversations about disability and its related issues and then make sure everyone should get included. Making that is trying to core a culture of inclusiveness, generate confidence and make impact in the community. The important thing is to step into this question: What and why still causing the social bareers ? Perhaps finding the answer will bring up to light to issues including inclusion.
