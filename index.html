But how can an inclusive environment be identified (detected) ? These are summarily some exploring points consisting not only to observing and questioning about how people in a chosen community are dealing with inclusion. After these steps answers and solutions should come as results of oberving and questioning.

1-A. Observations and questions about inclusion in local mentalities
In a chosen locality (area, region, place, zone, community) one of the simplistic ways to measure the level of INCLUSION is:
I. To observe and then
II. To question
For examples how things are happening there in terms of socializing, connections and interactions between people ? What is the nature of their connections and conversations ? Are there many, few people with difference or disabled ? What could be the reasons ? Why and how connections, interactions and communications can stand as leverages for people to learn more about inclusiveness in a locality ? How vulnerable people like elders, disabled people or people with difference are treated in the locality if they are living in ? Do and how non-disabled people interact with disabled people in communities ? In person ? By postal or virtual way like cellphones, internet or other ways ? What is the level of respect and consideration that non-disabled people are showing up to disabled people ?

2-B. Answers can lead to better informations about inclusion
Gathering answers from questions and then analyze them deeply and rationally in order to stick up a general view about INCLUSION in the chosen community. Probably the answers will emphasize an idea and gives an index to more learn and understand about how inclusion is dealt in the area. But something is clear, for any living community, the existence of connections with comnunications between people, supporting each-other-spirit, is a huge vector to get the community vibrant and to moving ahead in solidarity and cohesion. So there maybe a set of social rules to define how communities are organised to get governed. Leaders of communities have to work hard to keep the commnunity safe in its entirety meaning that disabled people should be fairly treated and included.

3-C. Communities, their members and their families matter
There is no doubt that many members want to build families, raise their children, make social connections and may have good life. And that, they have to respect their communities rules. At the same time, talking to each other, respecting neighbours, being in solidarity with each other or with those who are especially in need, behaving as social norms do recommand, is really imperative, fundamental and worthy to make livable communities. Also interacting and/or helping people in need no matter their cultural or physical difference is a great human value and inclusive because every human-being matters. It is how things should work in every community. It is a way to continue to build a strong and inclusive community where everyone is represented. The act of observing can be easier ou difficult but is feasable by anyone even it can take a time to come to a conclusion. The fact to be able to observe and idealize some causes of exclusion or inclusion is important and may lead to a self-making conclusion.

4-D. A volunteering And Ojective Mindset Of A Good Observer
It's also a good beginning to get familiar to local inclusion trend but no everyone can do it without a volunteering and objective mind. A good observer should make a special analysis about conversations between non-disabled and disabled people because it uses to be unfamiliar. It is some kind of interactions between people of difference and the rest of the society that will more highlight the level of Inclusion in the community. What constant and consistent those interactions are ? Can they impact positively the processus of inclusion for disabled people ? How disability is generally perceived in African countries especially in Burkina Faso, Niger or Mali all of them in the Sahel region of West Africa ?
It's obvious the existence of a little difference of development between the cited countries, the matters and issues tied to disabled people are approximatelly the same in Africa in terms of how people are living better or not with their disabilities. What are the feelings of disabled people about their rights?

5-E. The Main Focus Is To Dig Into Existential Specter Of Discriminations And Exclusion
The main focus is to dig deep into many subjects and concerns and getting tools to understand why there is a continual and discriminative specter of wrongdoing towards people with difference. What matters is the possibility of having more people to make substancial approaches and ideas of what it means disability and how disabled people are dealing with it on a daily basis.
Using resources, methologies, analyses in order to bring to light many issues and try to figure them out. After the telling of stories and narratives, it is so important to interview both of a non-disabled and disabled people to get their own views about disability and inclusion.

Immigration Is Heartbreaking
As a physical disabled person who legally made it to Canada, I experienced hardness and challenges on my way and still experiencing them on a daily basis like to reading a list of endless integers in an array. In addition to racism, discrimination and overlooking amongst many other issues for a newcomer, seeking asylum is a long and difficult process to reach. It matters with hard work, rigor, ponctuality, credibility, optimism and a high level of dose of patience to deal with longer and administrative procedures fueled by a culture of bureaucracy that can last at least after many years (3-7 years) before getting everything fixed. It's been so tough because as a newcomer in a new country, I have to embrace a new reality of living in addition to mine in order to have a good integration. I had neither parents, nor relatives in Montreal at this time. So I have to find out a way and place to stay. After spending 3 days in my motel room my finances have been hit. Thereby I've had to look for a cheap room to live. But how to do it as a newcomer in one of the coldest countries around the world on an upcoming winter ? Something was sure, I couldn't afford to sleep outside. Then I went to OBM shelter in downtown Montreal. There I spent a half year and then I moved to "La Maison du Pre" to wait for my hearing. I have successfully defended my case and got a grant. That's why I want to share some of my experiences with you in an upcoming book.

The vision of Tiki N'Yiki is promoted by Gabriel, a physical disabled person from Burkina Faso, a West African country. He attended his college degree at Bassy high school in Ziniar and then at Newton Descartes high school in Ouagadougou. He holds a Patent of Undergraduate Studies (P.U.S) called B.E.P.C [Brevet d'Etude du Premier Cycle].
As a fervent defender of Disability Rights, Gabriel knew he has to stand up and get involved within activities about disablity and corollary matters in implementing his ideas into practical realisations that can positively change lives. That's why he created the Centre PAAS NEERE for EDUCATION in 2006. Ideas are sometimes common even complexe and deep-thinking, but those linked frontally to disability and rights, are focal subjects of interest for Gabriel.

The need to get results in terms of social impacts through communities where disabled people are living, was a huge fuel and has sharpened the dynamism of Gabriel on his desire to make better lives for disabled and orphaned children in Burkina Faso. This is a way to promote, defend and protect Disability Rights and legacies disabled activists have reached, especially the right to get educated and be normally health-carried no matter who you are.

In 2006 ... Gabriel was one of the founding fathers of PAAS NEERE, an educational organisation in which he taught disabled and orphaned children but also led several awareness sessions with a focal theme: Disability and Social Inclusion.

In 2007, 2008, 2013, Gabriel went to Europe in order to promote his organisation PAAS NEERE and fundraise money for his projects including to improve education and provide reasonable health care services to disabled people in need. His stays (trips) led him not only to Switzerland like in Geneva, Sion, Delemont, and Vendlincourt through an impactful collaboration with patners and a network of friends and founding members of Transport Handicap in the canton of Valais but also in Germany at Rasttat and Kuppenheim in the lander of Bade-Wurtemberg [Baden-Wrttemberg] where he host a serie of conferences about PAAS NEERE the organisation of which he was president.

On december 11th, 2012 Gabriel was awarded as " Knight of the Order of Merit " by his country, Burkina Faso for his entire contribution and engagement in terms of being able to create, propel and maintain an inclusive environment for vulnerable people. Disabled and orphaned children are benefiting of his master-piece and the centre Paas Neere is like an harbor for them to get better lives through education.

In 2016 he created Tiki N'Yiki to expand more support for orphaned and marginalized children. On July 14, 2016, Gabriel was invited by generous friends to the United States of America for four (4) months to not only networking but also to selling his artworks [Paintings & Batiks] and fundraise money for projects.

From East Coast [ New York City-NY, Boston-MA, Appleton-ME, Burlington & St Johnsbury-VT] to West Coast [ Portland-OR, Olympia-WA] of the United States of America, with a huge support of friends, Gabriel was not only able to present his vision-project of Tiki N'Yiki during festivals and/or fairs but also talked in person with some people about realities of what it means to be disabled in his country, Burkina Faso given some exclusive mentalities and existential discriminations. As future perspectives, his idea is to build a wider network of collaborators and partners gathered around a foundation to work hard, achieve projects, support empowerment and social inclusion for disabled people.

In 2017, unfortunately for security reasons, Gabriel has left his country to fly to Canada . In fact, some of Tiki N'Yiki activities, including EDUCATION, awareness on disability, have been temporarily stopped but will be redefined if the conditions of security are met.
He is rebuilding a new life but with a ceaseless desire to not give up his vision of Tiki Yiki. Among other things, he is writting his memoirs, learning to code while hoping to attend Informations and Technologies studies. He is passionate on programming and is developing this website to let well-known his vision.

Gabriel likes family, nature, photography, philosophy, painting, coding, history, geography, architecture, cyclism, European soccer and hockey on ice.

A Survey On Disability And Rights

1. Do you think disabled people should be considered as normal people ?
2. Do you think disabled people should have the same rights like non-disabled people?
3. If yes what could be those rights ? If no, why ?
4. If you do consider that disabled people should fully have their rights, what are the first ones very important?
5. Do you have a close relative or know a disabled person you can interact with ?
6. Did you already interact with him or her?
7. If yes what was the subject of your conversation ? If no, why ?
8. If yes have you been impacted after your conversation?
9. What did impact you during your conversation?
10. Did you learn something important after your conversation?
11. Have you once done something to help a disabled person you know or not ?
12. Do you believe that disabled people are not important to building and harmonizing communities ?
13. What should be done to empower their lives?
14. Did you hear about the courage and the willingness of disabled people to stand up before any challenge and sometimes have achieved?
15. Did you already witness discriminations toward disabled people? If yes, what was the kind of discrimination ?
16. Have you intentionally or unintentionally discriminated a disabled person one day ? If yes how did you feel later ?
17. Do you know a disabled person who succeeded in his lifetime? What was his profession or job ?
18. Can you list a wide range of services you will have to implement for empowering lives of disabled people if you become a president-elect of your country one day ?
19. How will you implement your vision to empower lives of disabled people of your country ?
20. Who will you choose as part of a working team to get your vision done ? - 1* A team with only disabled people? - 2* A team without disabled people? - 3* Both?
