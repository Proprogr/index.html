What it means to live with a disability ?

Living with a disability is very learning. In one hand disabled people have to experience harder adversities and have no plans to come on. In the other hand they have to dynamically be creative and get to adapt/adjust in order to continue to live wherever the region is. And luckily Mother Nature let them fastly upgrade. But over the countless discriminations on a daily basis many disabled people are struggling at least two times harder to fulfill. This because of a toxic and exclusive mindset that is wholy misconceiving the image of disabled people. Disabled are sometimes portrayed as negative, non-productive, non-impacting people etc. But this is to arbritrarily barring them from opportunities and then realising their cheer dreams. It's been heard countless time that disabled people are good for nothing. This is to wrongly painting negativity into disability. Unfortunately the kind of propaganda is regular and is formatted into the public mindset. If people want to know about disability, they can do it. How can you do DEAR USER help promote and defend for rights of people living with disabilities ? Something has to get done because inclusion has not yet room for every disabled person everywhere. You may be one of the game-changers.

Etymology and Meaning

Tiki N'Yiki is etymologically structured of two (2) words of Moore, a language in Burkina Faso :

1. Tiki is litteraly meaning Leaning upon

2. N'Yiki is meaning Getting up

Why Tiki N'Yiki ?

Tiki N'Yiki is created to improve education and support efforts of disabled children in Burkina Faso. It is sounding as a slogan adressing the necessity and the possibility for everyone to lead his own achievement. Tiki N'Yiki stimulatively supports the idea that every disabled kid can have a better future if helped.

Region of Implementation

Tiki N'Yiki has first been implemented in Burkina Faso in 2016. Amongs many reasons is the main one that focuses to create a free and ideal space and time on improving education for disabled kids.
