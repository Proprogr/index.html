Why Tiki Yiki ? What Is Tiki Yiki Meaning ? What Is Tiki Yiki Doing ?

In Burkina Faso as everywhere there is a lot of challenges about inclusiveness. Getting "differently able people especially disabled people" included in communities is not regular on the daily basis. But it's so important to give them opportunities even their disabilities. Some organisations are steadily working to get it done including to be trully inclusive.

"Inclusiveness" May Be Ordinary Or New To You. But Inclusiveness As Word Never Been Strange To The Dictionary. That Means Words Have Been With Us Since The Creation. It Belongs To Human-Beings To Put Them Into Life By Practices. That's Why Inclusive Education Matters Because It's Not A Privilege But A Right For (Disabled Ones) Kids With Difference.

Inclusive Education has no room everywhere. It should not be like a privilege but an intrinseque right.

Climate Change Is a Big Threat To Earth. It's Increasingly Causing Deadly Floodings, Wildfires and Droughts Around The World. Welcome in Burkina Faso

Burkina Faso is a landlocked country nested in the heart of West Africa. Its capital and main city is Ouagadougou. The population of Burkina Faso is largely composed by young people and is about 20.9 millions people according to the World Bank recent stats. Geography influences Economy. The economy of Burkina Faso is mostly based on the agriculture, livestock breeding, mining, market gardening. The informal sector is believed to create many jobs for people. Citizens are called Burkinabè.

Immigration Is Heartbreaking
As a physical disabled person who have legally migrated to Canada, I have encountered many challenges. First of all seeking asylum is not an easy thing. It matters with long administrative procedures that can take at least five years long before getting the citizenship if granted. It's been hard because I am in a new country and have to embrace a new life and a new culture. I did have neither parents, nor relatives in Quebec-Canada. After spending 3 days in my motel room my finances have been hit and I have had to look for other ways especially a cheap room to live in. How to do as a newcomer in a cold country during automn? One thing was sure, I couldn't afford to sleep outside. So I went to Montreal downtown shelter to explain my situation and ask for help. Fortunately I have been granted asylum and then applied for the permanent residency's application. That, I've gotten certain informations and wanted to share them with you if one day have to deal with immigration in a similar way. Learn more...
