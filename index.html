Etymology and Meaning of Tiki Yiki

Tiki Yiki is etymologically structured with two (2) words [Tiki and Yiki] of [Mossi] language from Burkina Faso in West-Africa. Tiki means to LEAN ON and Yiki means to GET UP.

Literally translated, Tiki Yiki means to lean on and then get up. But philosophically and in a context of disability, Tiki Yiki means Disability is not Destiny.

A Philosophical View Of Tiki Yiki "From a philosophical view to a specifical-based and inclusive case like Disability, Tiki Yiki is echoeing efforts, courage and determination in fulfilling own goals. Basically Yiki Yiki means to focus on auto-determination with ceaseless efforts."
For many disabled people this has been more harder than nobody else in terms of aspiring to autonomy. Nothing is easy and not garantued to those people in Burkina Faso even a periodically welfare pay to help them insure daily basic need. In that reality gettinf educated, having a good care and having a food security is advantageously empowering and life-changing. But many disabled and orphaned kids in rural campaigns of Burkina Faso are still living into that specter of worries and there is little to do to make things better in deep villages.

Stammering Inclusion In Burkina Faso

In Burkina Faso, despite the increasing number of the population of disabled people (From polio or other diseases and elders), we are still living sometimes scenarios where disabled people are completely underestimated. In the civil code few policies are set nationwide to value and protect the rights of that social component in inclusive way. And those policies that have been implemented never practically get set up. They (policies ) have been limited only to theorical texts and empty speeches. How much time should the disabled population wait before totally grabbing what are called their rights? The right to live decently even the disability? The state of inclusion for disabled children is still stammering and unexistant in some regions around Africa. Culminated situation as reject hit regularly many disabled people who are countlessly experiencing in many ways discriminations, social injustice because first of all their disabilities and second the mentalities that are underestimating disabled people. There is a big concern about understanding, accepting, socially including and helping people with disabilities. Therefore living with disability in the societies with such mentalities is really and perpetually threatening no matter the gender in terms of having acceptable quality of life. Some of those disabled people feel sometimes like second-class citizens and they can meet no opportunities because they're being discriminated and forgotten. The fact that people who supposed to get support have come to be forgotten is unbelievably heartbreaking. It seems to be a social mistake and should immediately be fought.

Then any project in the future for disabled people will be eased, rise optimism, hope and bring positive change to disabled population than the fear and social discriminations they're experiencing on a daily basis. Disabled kids have to be promoted by inclusion, so that they can meet some opportunities and get a certain autonomy or semi-autonomy in the future. These are crucial goals Tiki Yiki focuses on to be able to accompaign especially disabled kids who need to reach their full potentials but are generally isolated because of social discriminations.

A Lack Of Adapted Infrastructures And Services
One of the common characteristics of African countries is that they do not sufficiently have adapted and coordinated services to support education and inclusion of people living with disabilities in general but especially to disabled children. And this makes them excluded from a social participation to the development. They are still put away from the community. It's unclear that living in countries with non inclusive mentalities, disabled kids will be easily included as kids like others.

Why Tiki Yiki ?
Given that appalant mentioned reality below in terms of including disabled people in Burkina Faso, it's obvious they experience social discriminations on a daily basis. Depending the area they're living, a few has a chance to attend school and may dream.

It's like easy for some people to make kids but very heavy for them to take responsabilities. When it's kids with disabilities that's a lot of difficulties and that's why Tiki Yiki has been founded in 2016 to focus on those different issues met by kids with disablities. The issues are diveses, thoughest at a different level but are also challengeable and the society has tools to come to end the discriminations toward difference in Burkina Faso wher I come from. Meanwhile it can look like inthinkable the fact to see many disabled not being able to go to hospital or to a care centre because not having the possibiltiy. No car, no moto, no bike and being diabled and livng in a remote area where it rare to regularly transportation. It's rural. So based on those facts Tiki Yiki would define, implement and facilitate lives to many of those disabled people including kids who're living in deep villages in Burkina Faso. The project would found tricycles (A three weels cycle for disabled people) to help them hanging out where they need to. There are obviously education, health care, food security and climate change challenges. Because of the insufficience of adapetd infractrustures and services, schools or hospitals, Tiki Yiki plans to build a multi-service and adapted centre (With a medical centre where some volunteers, all around the world, can offer their carrying services) to disabled people especially children and orphans and also the local population.

For those whose families are living in remote areas, rooms and other infractrustures will be built in the the centre yard for them. Doing that, kids will conveniently live in adapted and secure places but are allowed to go back to visit their families according to a plan. Living at Tiki Yiki's centre that is built in a city means to be close to some important, useful, daily services and needs like hospitals, shops, sport centres or/and others.

They may at least experience less discriminations than in rural areas where inclusion for disabled children has no means to them. Tiki Yiki will support them to figure it out and then change a social mentality about disability. We all know the key importance of education as a powerful tool for life, the main goal of Tiki Yiki is to improve education, empower lives of disabled children and orphans by giving them the opportunities they deserve even their disabilities. That's why INCLUSION for disabled children should also be supported.

Having the right to get educated, carried and obviously be safe from food insecurity is part of the vision that Tiki Yiki is projecting on.

The Climate Change Approach The fourth point to have been taken in count is the CLIMATE CHANGE and its increasing threats to the environment and obviously to us as human-beings. Tiki Yiki has plan to make sure that the different challenges against Climate Change can get met. But Tiki Yiki already organized cleaning days with the neighborhood kids to help sensibilize the public opinion about climate change and drive the necessity for everyone to do something for the proctection of the nature. Climate Change is impacting our lives day by day but human-being is the real factor of the climate change. That's why through kids Tiki Yiki is sending a strong message of and needs them to understand the issues and then get involved.

A Bit Of History

Basically the envy to help others has originated the vision of TIKI YIKI during the year of 2016 when I was living in Burkina Faso, my homeland country.

Before I worked as an organizer in an association of disabled kids and orphans where I started to nurture the idea of Tiki Yiki. The motivation seems to be innate in me. So when I shared my idea and motivation with friends living around the world, some of them appreciated and congratulated me.

Many of them had let me know they can support the interesting vision not only by small donations but also in networking to fundraise money and support the project. It was the beginning of a dream.

The Seem-To-Be-Seen Case Of Kuirba From Tabinko
There are lots people who have done so much things to help people living with disabilities and sharing their services and love to those people.

While some people continue on the way of their capabilities to support as well. Some people, organisations or/and associations are interestied to donate for foundations, organisations of resarch working in Disability Matters (Areas of Research). Many people invest themselves to support disabled and/or elders every time. That kind of commitments is really welcomed.

But there are also other disabled people who are cruallly lacking a simple visite, a warmful hi. The need to hang out but can't no possibility. No autonomy....

Every day, Indoors seems to attract monotony and leaves you like in prison. Get out there is his hymn. But no one for him. It loks crazy. Really crazy. I can't imagine how tougher is the life of someone is elder, disabled and living lonely while. Something is sure. It's not easy because living is to do many things despite himself (His disability).

Mr Kuirba as a disabled elder has a delicate and heartbreaking life story. He's living lonely in a remote village of TABINKO. But at the same time his experiencing life-story is also a teaching case that can let everyone lear about what is to live the solitude?

What is the level it can make life boring and desperated especially someone living with disability like elder Tabinko. Even if our courage makes us feeling strong as people with disabilities and we are aware to live with that the rest of the life, it' s also a kind a motivating factor that push many to succed. is to experience a kind of life during a long time. Despite the tougher living-conditons, Kuirba is a guy full of humour and that is his passion. He likes to make people laugh, feel comfortable and relaxed. He is disabled and ever got neither a wife nor a kid in his lifetime, pretty funy. But he likes to be surrounded by children, talk and obviously play with them as a kid. He likes to help people figuring out of their issues. What's a wonderful man. He usually tells people this: Listen, when I make you laugh I am happy because you are happy too. But something you don't know is what happening to me during some nights when it is aboundantely raining. I have no light, the darkened nights become louder and I am suffering of that. Especially when during rainfalls. I can't sleep, I spent time to tinking and thinking. Every rainy night brings up a diligence of issues The unability to run as other kids, jumping trees, playing foot ball is appalant. The disabled older man Kuirba living in the remote village of TABINKO suffers of mistreatments, negligence. When I lived in Burkina Faso, I visited countless times many disabled kids in rural areas in order to support them and learn about their realities, living-conditions that are really rude, worrying given the huge lack of wheelchairs, crutches and tricycles to have a mobility. There are more isolated and few people worried about them including some of their families.

For many disabled people in Africa and especially in my home country Burkina Faso, every day is the same. Some of them are living lonely, loosing hope then have no more energy. Their morale has come down because the social stigmas. I felt angry to see that diabled kids are ceaselessly mistreatened, discriminated and forgetten. Experiencing what they're living every single day let me realize that I have to do something to change. I couldn't estimate what they were living, suffering, but I am sure their pain is hugely uncalculable and traumatizing. Having experienced such mistreatments and discriminations in Burkina Faso (Africa) I knew it means to be disabled. It's heartbreaking when you see how they are uncompted and underestimated.

In some rural areas I am aware of what some families don't like their disabled parents or relatives to being associated with their celebrations. It's like those disabled are denigrating their families. So there are some people who hide their handicapped relatives, not allowing them to show up during families parties, meetings, celebrations, weddings, baptisms or funerals that gather many people. That's seems to be unrealistic to lots people nowadays. It's crual, unhuman and heartbreaking. Especially disabled kids who have the same rights like the physically ones to get educated. That's why I thought to those issues and which action is a right to do and then leading to fundamental changes about social perceptions to disabled people and especially the disabled kids. living with disabilities. I am looking forward to support the community live better, together in total harmony. Empowering lives of many disabled kids and/or orphans in Burkina Faso is to get done.
